Preparing for the Australian Citizenship Test can feel daunting, especially if you aren't entirely sure how the exam is scored. A common question among applicants is: *What is the exact pass mark for the Australian Citizenship Test, and how is it calculated?*

In this comprehensive guide, we break down everything you need to know about the pass mark for 2024/2025, the critical "Zero Tolerance" rule for the Australian Values section, and exactly what it takes to pass on your first attempt.

## The Basic Math: The 75% Rule

The Australian Citizenship Test is a computer-based, multiple-choice exam consisting of **20 questions**. 

To pass the test overall, you must achieve a score of **75% or higher**. This means you need to answer at least **15 out of the 20 questions correctly**. 

While 75% might seem like a manageable benchmark, the test is not just a straightforward numbers game. In late 2020, the Department of Home Affairs introduced a significant change that completely shifted how the pass mark is applied.

## The Catch: The "Zero Tolerance" Australian Values Section

Of the 20 questions on your test, exactly **five questions** will focus exclusively on Australian values. These cover topics like freedom of speech, mutual respect, equality of men and women, and the importance of democracy.

Here is the most critical rule you must remember: **You must answer all five Australian values questions correctly to pass the test.**

Even if you score 100% on the history, geography, and government sections—getting 19 out of 20 overall—if the *one* question you get wrong is an Australian values question, **you will fail the entire test**. 

This "zero tolerance" policy is why many otherwise well-prepared applicants find themselves failing.

## How the Questions are Distributed

While the exact questions vary for every applicant (they are pulled randomly from a large database), the structure of the test remains consistent:

1. **Australia and its People:** Questions about Indigenous Australians, early settlement, and national traditions.
2. **Australia's Democratic Beliefs, Rights and Liberties:** This is where the crucial Australian Values questions are sourced.
3. **Government and the Law in Australia:** Questions regarding how the parliamentary system works, voting, and the legal system.

## What Happens if You Don't Reach the Pass Mark?

If you score below 15 out of 20, or if you get a values question wrong, you will fail the test. 

Fortunately, failing does not mean your journey to citizenship is over. You can generally retake the test on the same day if the testing centre has availability, or you can book another appointment. There is no additional fee for retaking the test.

However, failing can cause significant delays in your application processing time, adding weeks or even months to your wait.
